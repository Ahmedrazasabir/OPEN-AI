OpenAI s text embeddings measure the relatedness of text strings. Embeddings are commonly use for.
Search (where results are ranked by relevance to a query string)
Clustering (where text strings are grouped by similarity)
Recommendations (where items with related text strings are recommended)
Anomaly detection (where outliers with little relatedness are identified)
Diversity measurement (where similarity distributions are analyzed)
Classification (where text strings are classified by their most similar label)

Semantic Similarity:

Words with similar meanings are often represented by vectors that are close together in the embedding space. For example, "king" and "queen" would have similar embeddings because they are related concepts.

Syntactic Similarity:

Word embeddings also capture syntactic relationships between words. For instance, the vectors for "walked" and "walking" might be close together because they are different forms of the same verb.

Algebraic Operations:

Word embeddings exhibit interesting algebraic properties. For example, subtracting the vector for "man" from "king" and adding "woman" often results in a vector close to the vector for "queen", demonstrating analogical reasoning capabilities.
