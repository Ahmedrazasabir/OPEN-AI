Prompt engineering

This guide shares strategies and tactics for getting better results from large language models (sometimes referred to as GPT models) like GPT-4. The methods described here can sometimes be deployed in combination for greater effect. We encourage experimentation to find the methods that work best for you.
Using OpenAI's Prompt engineering models, you can build applications to:
Include details in your query to get more relevant answers
Ask the model to adopt a persona
Use delimiters to clearly indicate distinct parts of the input
Specify the steps required to complete a task
Provide examples
Specify the desired length of the output

Clarity:

Prompts should clearly communicate the desired task or context to the model, guiding it to produce relevant responses.

Specificity:

Prompts should be specific enough to encourage the model to generate responses that meet the user's needs or expectations.

Length:

The length of the prompt can influence the quality and relevance of the model's response. In some cases, concise prompts may be more effective, while in others, longer prompts may provide additional context.

Examples:

Providing examples of desired responses or formatting can help the model better understand the prompt's expectations.

Fine-tuning:

In some cases, fine-tuning the model on specific prompts or tasks can improve its performance in generating relevant responses.
